# A Web-Based Human Resource Management System for LTO Pila, Laguna

## TITLE 1
A Web-Based Human Resource Management System for LTO Pila, Laguna with CSC Forms, SALN, and Personnel Data Sheet Modules

## I. INTRODUCTION

In modern government offices, managing personnel records and compliance documents has become increasingly complex. The Land Transportation Office (LTO) in Pila, Laguna, like many other government agencies, handles a large volume of employee information, including Civil Service Commission (CSC) forms, Personnel Data Sheets (PDS), and Statements of Assets, Liabilities, and Net Worth (SALN). Traditionally, these records have been maintained manually, which often leads to inefficiencies, misplaced documents, and delays in processing requests.

To address these challenges, this project proposes the development of a Web-Based Human Resource Management System specifically designed for LTO Pila, Laguna. The system aims to digitize and automate the management of employee records, allowing authorized HR personnel to easily access, update, and generate required documents. By integrating CSC forms, PDS, and SALN modules into a single platform, the system ensures that all personnel data is secure, accurate, and readily available.

The implementation of this system will improve the overall efficiency of the HR processes at LTO Pila by reducing manual work, minimizing errors, and streamlining document tracking. Furthermore, it supports government compliance by providing an organized and transparent way of managing employee information. This documentation provides a detailed explanation of the system’s design, features, and workflow, serving as a guide for both users and developers in understanding how the system functions and contributes to a more efficient HR management process.

## II. RATIONALE OF THE STUDY

Human resource management is a vital component of any organization, including government agencies such as the Land Transportation Office (LTO) in Pila, Laguna. The effective handling of personnel records, CSC forms, Personnel Data Sheets (PDS), and Statements of Assets, Liabilities, and Net Worth (SALN) is essential not only for administrative efficiency but also for ensuring compliance with government regulations. Traditionally, these documents have been processed manually, which often results in delays, misplaced records, and errors. Such inefficiencies can hinder the day-to-day operations of the HR office and affect the timely submission of required reports and compliance documents.

The rationale for this study is to address these challenges by developing a Web-Based Human Resource Management System tailored to the specific needs of LTO Pila, Laguna. By digitizing employee records and automating the processing of CSC forms, PDS, and SALN submissions, the system provides a centralized and secure platform for HR personnel. This reduces the reliance on manual record-keeping, minimizes the risk of errors, and accelerates administrative tasks.

Moreover, the system supports transparency and accountability by ensuring that all personnel information is accurately recorded and easily retrievable. It also enhances decision-making, as HR managers can generate reports and track employee compliance efficiently. Ultimately, this study aims to provide a practical, technology-driven solution that improves the management of government personnel records, promotes operational efficiency, and strengthens adherence to civil service regulations.

## III. RESEARCH OBJECTIVES

### General Objectives
- To develop a Web-Based Human Resource Management System for LTO Pila, Laguna that automates the management of personnel records, including CSC forms, Personnel Data Sheets (PDS), and Statements of Assets, Liabilities, and Net Worth (SALN), in order to improve efficiency, accuracy, and compliance in human resource operations.

### Specific Objectives
- To digitize employee profiles and personnel information for easier access, updates, and storage.
- To automate the generation and tracking of CSC forms, ensuring timely submission and reducing manual errors.
- To manage and monitor SALN submissions annually, providing an organized and secure record of assets and liabilities.
- To provide a centralized database that allows HR personnel to quickly retrieve records, generate reports, and track compliance.
- To enhance security and accessibility of personnel records through role-based access and secure login features.
- To streamline the overall HR process, reducing manual workload, paperwork, and delays in document processing.

## IV. SCOPE AND LIMITATIONS

### Scope
This study focuses on the development of a Web-Based Human Resource Management System for LTO Pila, Laguna. The system is designed to digitize and automate the management of personnel records and government-required documents, specifically Personnel Data Sheets (PDS), Civil Service Commission (CSC) forms, and Statements of Assets, Liabilities, and Net Worth (SALN).

The system allows authorized HR personnel to register and maintain employee profiles, generate and track CSC forms, monitor annual SALN submissions, and generate reports for administrative and compliance purposes. It provides a centralized platform that ensures quick access, accurate data management, and secure storage of sensitive employee information.

By focusing on these core functions, the system aims to reduce manual work, improve efficiency, and enhance the overall management of HR operations in LTO Pila, Laguna.

### Limitations
Despite its intended benefits, this system has certain limitations. Access is restricted to authorized HR personnel and administrators, meaning regular employees cannot directly modify their records. Being web-based, the system relies on a stable internet connection, and any disruptions may affect accessibility.

The study is limited to the management of CSC forms, PDS, and SALN; other HR processes such as payroll, recruitment, or employee benefits are not included. Additionally, while the system automates the generation of forms, official review and approval by authorized personnel are still required. Lastly, migration of existing paper-based records into the system is not covered; it assumes that all initial employee data will be manually encoded.

## V. RESEARCH LOCALE

The study will be conducted at the Land Transportation Office (LTO) located in Pila, Laguna. LTO Pila is a branch of the national Land Transportation Office, which is responsible for implementing policies, programs, and services related to the licensing, registration, and regulation of motor vehicles in the Philippines. In addition to these primary functions, the office also handles administrative tasks, including the management of human resources, employee records, and compliance with government regulations.

The LTO Pila branch was chosen as the research locale because it represents a typical government office that handles a large volume of personnel information. Currently, many of its HR processes, including the maintenance of Civil Service Commission (CSC) forms, Personnel Data Sheets (PDS), and Statements of Assets, Liabilities, and Net Worth (SALN), are handled manually. This manual system often leads to inefficiencies, misplaced documents, and delays in processing, making it an ideal setting to implement and evaluate a digital HR management system.

By conducting the study at LTO Pila, Laguna, the researchers aim to address specific challenges faced by the office and demonstrate the potential benefits of automating HR processes, including improved efficiency, accuracy, and compliance with government requirements. This locale also provides a real-world environment for testing the system, ensuring that it meets the needs of HR personnel while aligning with government standards.

## VI. TARGET RESPONDENTS

The target respondents of this study are the authorized personnel and staff of the Land Transportation Office (LTO) Pila, Laguna, particularly those involved in human resource management and administrative tasks. These include HR officers, administrative staff, and other employees who are responsible for managing personnel records, processing Civil Service Commission (CSC) forms, Personnel Data Sheets (PDS), and Statements of Assets, Liabilities, and Net Worth (SALN).

These respondents were chosen because they are the primary users of the proposed system and will directly benefit from its features. Their daily tasks involve updating employee information, generating and tracking government-required forms, monitoring compliance, and maintaining accurate records. By gathering insights, feedback, and input from these individuals, the study aims to design a system that is practical, user-friendly, and aligned with the actual needs of HR personnel.

Furthermore, these respondents will participate in the testing and evaluation of the system, providing valuable information regarding its functionality, usability, and effectiveness. Engaging the actual users ensures that the system addresses real-world challenges faced in the HR processes of LTO Pila, Laguna, and helps guarantee that it will improve efficiency, accuracy, and compliance in personnel management.
